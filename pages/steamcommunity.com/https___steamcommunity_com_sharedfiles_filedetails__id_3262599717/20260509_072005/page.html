About the Archetype
Base Class - Arcanist

Archetype Description - Some learn magic to gain power, others in pursuit of a higher purpose. A magic deceiver is one who has learned to fool magic itself, combining known spells to devise entirely new forms of manipulation.

Spellbook casting attribute - Charisma

Features:

  • Magic Fusion -
    At 1st level, a magic deceiver gains the ability to create fused spells, combining the effects of two regular spells from her spellbook. She doesn't receive bonus spells from ability scores and cannot add spells from other classes' spell lists to her spellbook. Due to the divergence between their principles of magic use, a magic deceiver cannot take the mystic theurge or loremaster classes.

    The magic deceiver gives each fused spell a name and writes it into her spellbook. A fused spell gains the effects of both initial spells. The effects of the two initial spells are appolied in order, one after the other.

    A fused spell gains all descriptors of the initial spells, has a spell level equal to the highest level of the initial spells, is always cast as a standard action, and cannot be modified by metamagic. If the initial spells belong to different schools of magic, have different spell shapes, or require saves of different types to avoid their effects, when creating a fused spell, the magic deceiver defines these properties, choosing between the corresponding properties of the initial spells. The choice of the spell's shape also determines its range, targets, and whether or not it is affected by the target's spell resistance (if the initial spells have the same shape, the best options are chosen for these properties). If any effects of a fused spell require its target to make saving throws, the target always makes a single saving throw, excluding cases where it is required to make another save after a failed save (such as the phantasmal killer spell).

    The magic deceiver can have up to 10 fused spells in her spellbook at a time.

    If the effects applied by a fused spell have limits on the maximum number of damage dice, the fused spell receives the following limits instead:

    Spell level 1 - up to 5 damage dice
    Spell level 2 - up to 7 damage dice
    Spell level 3 - up to 10 damage dice
    Spell level 4 - up to 15 damage dice
    Spell level 5 - up to 20 damage dice
    Spell level 6 - unlimited damage dice


  • Ways of the Deceiver - Every magic deceiver finds their own way to fabricate magic to mislead others. Each such path alters their abilities and offers different means for achieving their ends.

Archetype Advantages and Disadvantages
Advantages
  • Magic Fusion
  • Ways of the Deceiver
  • Unique debuffs
  • Possible to pick up an animal companion
  • easily bypass spell resistance by fusing spells or from your Way.


Disadvantages
  • Maximum of Level 6 spells
  • Slow gain of spells per day
  • Low BAB
  • Not a full list of Arcane Spells
  • No metamagic feats
  • Cannot gain spells from any source
  • Cannot merge Mystic Theurge or Loremaster
Magic Fusion Guide and Fusion Tips
Magic Fusion is the main feature of the Magical Deceiver and does exactly as described on the tin. So lets get down to explaining it and its parts.

1. Magic Fusion - This replaces the metamagic tab in your spellbook disallowing access to metamagic feats and gives you a new menu called the Magic Fusion menu. Press it to enter the magic fusion menu.

2. Magical Fusion Spells - You have 10 slots with which to create Fusion Spells. Press the numbered button to change between your creations.

3. Spell Combination - These 2 boxes are the spells used to create your custom fused spell. The spell placed on the left is the one that hits the target first while the spell placed on the right is the spell that hits the target second.

4. Name your spell - This textbox is to give your spell a custom name.

5. School of Magic - The schools available to choose from. These are chosen based on the 2 spells chosen for fusion.

6. Saving Throw - The saving throws available for your fused spell. This only applies the spells in the fusion that already had saving throws.

7. Spell Shape - These are how your spell works for targeting. Single means it will target a single enemy. Line means it will be cast in a line. Cone as a cone. Burst is a circular area that can be the size of one of the fused spells radii(Size and determination are based on the highest rank of spell. For example Fireball vs Banshee Blast will use the Banshee Blast radii or Wracking Ray+chain lightning will use chain lightning's single target selection rather than the ranged touch attack due to banshee and chain both being higher in spell level.).

8. Write Spell - This completes the finished spell placing it in your spellbook at the spell level of the highest fused spell. For example if you use phantasmal killer and fireball this fused spell is level 5.


To delete a fused spell simply create a new one in the same slot it will replace the current spell in that slot.

Tips:

  • Having one spell in the fusion not effected by spell resistance makes the entire spell bypass spell resistance.
  • Order does not matter when it comes to DC checks as the spell is one check.
  • If saving throws are used make sure to use a spell school you are focused in for best saving throws. Though choosing a save that is much lower may also be beneficial.
  • If both spells require an attack roll then the fused spell will also require one, where as if only one spell requires an attack roll it will not require one.
Ways of the Deceiver(Fake Celebrity)

You impersonate a celebrity - a famous actor,performer. You have learned a selection of their greatest pieces, which you can only use to weaken your enemies instead of empowering allies.


Inhibit Courage - A 1st-level fake celebrity can use her performance to rob enemies of their courage, making them more susceptible to fear and weakening their combat abilities. To be affected, an enemy must be able to perceive the fake celebrity's performance. An affected enemy receives a -1 penalty on saving throws against charm and fear effects and a -1 penalty on attack and weapon damage rolls. At 5th level, and every six arcanist levels therafter, this penalty increases by 1, to a maximum of -4 at 17th level.

The fake celebrity's arcanist level does not stack with her bard level for the purpose of determining the effects of this ability. The fake celebrity can use this ability for a number of rounds per day equal to her arcanist level + her charisma modifier.

Trick Magic - At 2nd level, a magic deceiver receives a bonus on concentration checks equal to the number of ranks she has in Persuasion.

Spell Fabrication - At 4th level, a magic deceiver gains the ability to fabricate a spell from her spellbook and cast it. To do so, she must succeed at a Persuasion (bluff) skill check. The DC of this check is equal to 10 + twice the spell's level. The magic deceiver can use this ability once per day at 4th level, plus one additional time per day for every four levels beyond 4th (to a maximum of five times per day at 20th level).

Inhibit Greatness - A fake celebrity of 12th level or higher can use her performance to reduce enemies' fighting capabilities. An affected target receives a -2 penalty on attack rolls, a -1 penalty on saving throws, and a -3 penalty on caster level checks made to overcome spell resistance. At 17th level, the penaltyon caster level checks increases to -4.

The fake celebrity's arcanist level does not stack with her bard level for the purpose of determining the effects of this ability, and she does not gain any bonuses from the Mythic Inspiration mythic ability. The fake celebrity can use this ability for a number of rounds per day equal to her arcanist level + her Charisma modifier.

Inhibit Heroics - A fake celebrity of 20th level can use her performance to make enemies look foolish and weak. An affected target receives a -4 penalty to AC and on saving throws.

The fake celebrity's arcanist level does not stack with her bard level for the purpose of determining the effects of this ability, and she does not gain any bonuses from the Mythic Inspiration mythic ability. The fake celebrity can use this ability for a number of rounds per day equal to her arcanist level + her charisma modifier.
Ways of the Deceiver(Living Deity)
You were born with a mystical gift and you use it for your own benefit. Following in the footsteps of Razmir, you have proclaimed yourself a living god to the foolish and gullible masses, spreading all manner of rumors about your alleged divinity.

Restrictions - Atheism


Mysterious Gift - At 1st level, a living deity gains the oracle's mystery and oracle's curse class features, but she receives no benefits from her curse through level progression. She can only select atheism when making her choice of deity. She uses Charisma instead of Wisdom while attempting Lore (religion) checks. She gains a revelation available to her mystery at 1st level, at 4th level, at 12th level, and at 20th level.

Pious Guile - At 2nd level, a living deity receives a bonus on concentration checks equal to the number of ranks she has in Lore (religion).

Godly Fraud - At 12th level, a living deity receives a bonus on caster level checks made to overcome spell resistance equal to half the number of ranks she has in Lore (religion).

Hollow Spark - At 20th level, a living deity is surrounded by so many rumors and lies that she attains powers that can be perceived as divine. She can choose 2 divine domains and gain any powers granted by those domains.
Ways of the Deceiver(Magic Inventor)
An avid experimenter, you have a way with magical items and carry with you an impressive collection to ensure that you always have the tools to dupe the odds.


Magic Inventions - At 1st level, a magic inventor receives a bonus on Use Magic Device skill checks equal to half her arcanist level.

Fake Enhancement - At 2nd level, any enhancement bonus granted to the magic inventor by an item increases by 1. If it is an enhancement bonus to an ability score, it increases by 2 instead.

Enchanter's Fabrication - At 4th level, a magic inventor gains the ability to fabricate a spell from her spellbook and cast it. To do so, she must succeed at a Use Magic Device skill check. The DC of this check is equal to 10 + twice the spell's level. The magic inventor can use this ability once per day at 4th level, plus one additional time per day for every four levels beyond 4th (to a maximum of five times per day at 20th level).

Enchanter's Penetration - At 12th level, a magic inventor receives a bonus on caster level checks made to overcome spell resistance equal to half the number of ranks she has in Use magic Device.

True Enhancement - At 20th level, the bonus granted by the magic inventor's fake enhancement ability increases to 2 (to 4 in the case of bonuses to ability scores).
Ways of the Deceiver(Razmiri Infiltrator)
As a trusted follower of Razmir, you infiltrate communities and undermine regimes, making it easier for your divine master to seize control of these territories whenever he may see fit to do so.

Restrictions - Atheist Only, Neutral or Evil only

Razmiri Mask - At 1st level, a Razmiri infiltrator gains a Razmiri mask. As she grows closer to the Living God Razmir, performing deeds in his name, her mask grants her ever-greater abilities. She cannot wear any other items on her face.

Acolyte of the First Step - At 1st level, the Razmiri infiltrator's mask makes her alignment undetectable and grants her a +2 circumstance bonus on Persuasion (bluff) checks.
Priest of the Third Step - At 3rd level, the mask grants her a +2 circumstance bonus on initiative checks.
Herald of the Eighth Step - At 8th level, the mask grants her a constant effect of the see invisibility spell.
Mask of the Twelvth Step - At 12th level, the mask grants her spell resistance equal to 12 + her arcanist level.
Vision of the Fifteenth Step - At 15th level, the mask grants her a constant effect of the true seeing spell and increases the DCs of all fused spells she casts by 2.

Trick Magic - At 2nd level, a magic deceiver receives a bonus on concentration checks equal to the number of ranks she has in Persuasion.

Spell Fabrication - At 4th level, a magic deceiver gains the ability to fabricate a spell from her spellbook and cast it. To do so, she must succeed at a Persuasion (bluff) skill check. The DC of this check is equal to 10 + twice the spell's level. The magic deceiver can use this ability once per day at 4th level, plus one additional time per day for every four levels beyond 4th (to a maximum of five times per day at 20th level).

Fool Resistance - At 12th level, a magic deceiver receives a bonus on caster level checks made to overcome spell resistance equal to half the number of ranks she in Persuasion.

At 20th level, a Razmiri infiltrator becomes so close to Razmir himself that he reveals to her secrets known only to a few. Once per round when an enemy within 30 feet of the Razmiri infiltrator casts a spell, she can attempt a Persuasion (bluff) skill check to counter that spell. The DC of this check is equal to the DC of the spell.
Ways of the Deceiver(Time Manipulator)

You exploit rare forms of magic to make time itself fall for your deceptions.

Advance Time - At 1st level, as a swift action, a time manipulator can speed up time for herself or her ally. The duration of all effects currently affecting the target reduces by a number of rounds equal to the time manipulator's level. The target does not make any saving throws and does not receive any negative effects for the skipped duration. The time manipulator can use this ability a number of times per day equal to 1 + her Charisma modifier.

Trick Magic - At 2nd level, a magic deceiver receives a bonus on concentration checks equal to the number of ranks she has in Persuasion.

Chronomancy - At 4th level, a time manipulator can restore all her or her ally's spells and abilities as if after a complete rest (without granting any other benefits of resting). Until the target rests properly, the DCs of all its spells and abilities will be decreased by 1 and it cannot be targeted by this ability again. This ability cannot be used in combat. The time manipulator can use this ability once per day at 4th level, plus one additional time per day for every four levels beyond 4th (to a maximum of five times per day at 20th level).

Split Second - At 12th level, a time manipulator can momentarily slow down the world around her to avoid danger. She can make a Persuasion (bluff) skill check instead of Reflex saving throw.

Split Timeline - At 20th level, a time manipulator can return herself or one of her allies back in time. The affected creature returns to the state they were in at the beginning of the day after a complete rest. They restore all their spells, abilities, and hit points and get rid of any effects which are removed on rest. This ability can only be used once per combat.
Ways of the Deceiver(Way of the Charlatan)
You make the most of your ability to bend the laws of magic.

Fool Magic - At 1st level, a magic deceiver can make Persuasion (bluff) skill checks instead of Use Magic Device checks.

Trick Magic - At 2nd level, a magic deceiver receives a bonus on concentration checks equal to the number of ranks she has in Persuasion.

Spell Fabrication - At 4th level, a magic deceiver gains the ability to fabricate a spell from her spellbook and cast it. To do so, she must succeed at a Persuasion (bluff) skill check. The DC of this check is equal to 10 + twice the spell's level. The magic deceiver can use this ability once per day at 4th level, plus one additional time per day for every four levels beyond 4th (to a maximum of five times per day at 20th level).

Fool Resistance - At 12th level, a magic deceiver receives a bonus on caster level checks made to overcome spell resistance equal to half the number of ranks she has in Persuasion.

Supreme Hoax - At 20th level, a magic deceiver becomes a legendary master of bluff. When she makes a Persuasion (bluff) check to cast a spell with her spell fabrication ability, the caster level and save DC of this spell increase by 2.
Which Way is right for me?
When choosing your way you have 6 options to choose from, but which selection is the correct choice? That is what we are here to figure out.

Each of the ways has specific instances on why they are worth picking and not picking so lets explain each option.

The fake celebrity is essentially a reverse bard where its performances negatively impact enemies rather than helping allies. This way does not receive benefits from Mythic Inspiration so its bonuses will remain static.

A living deity has access to mysteries and revelations making it the only Way to receive an animal companion. At level 20 it also gains access to 2 of any domain(Alignment domains are based on your alignment).

The magic inventor uses Use magic Device for its features as well as boosting any enhancement bonus found on an item. This Way receives bonuses to Fabrication which amounts to 5 bonus spells per day at level 20 and can use UMD as spell penetration at level 12.

Razmiri infiltrators have their mask which levels with them which means they cannot wear other hats such as charisma enhancing ones, but their hat provides true seeing later on, gives it spell resistance, and a slight bonus to initiative. The level 20 capstone provides a once per round counterspell that cancels the first spell an enemy caster per round(limited to you having it toggled on or off). Incredibly strong in the later game.

Time Manipulators this Way is both extremely good and lacking. It's amazing at it can double, triple, quadruple your team's spells/ability uses per day as early as level 4 as well as help remove short term debuffs. By level 20 you can reset a characters hp, spells per day, abilities per day and remove any negative/positive status effects afflicting them once per combat.

Way of the Charlatan stands out as basic but provides a strong synergy with persuasion. From level you can replace your UMD checks with persuasion checks. Gain concentration checks based on persuasion making it unlikely you'll fail the check, spell fabrication boosting your spells per day by +5 by level 20, and fool resistance which by level 12 lets you improve your spell penetration checks.

Early Game Ways
Living Deity - This one is a no contest early game being able to take an animal companion via the nature mystery is incredibly good.
Time Manipulator - This one lets you as early as level 4 reset your ability/spell uses per day avoiding a rest if you need more spells.

Midgame
Living Deity - This one stays strong throughout the game with the revelations and getting access to Godly Fraud letting you use your Lore (religion) skill as spell penetration.
Time Manipulator - This one continues resetting your spells, but lacks the spell penetration feature meaning you'll need to invest in spell penetration feats/gear.
Magic Inventor - This one starts to rise in the mid game with gear providing enhancement bonuses, and Enchanter's Penetration letting you pierce spell resistance.
Razmiri Infiltrator - This also starts getting good at he mid game with Fool resistance, and a stronger mask.
Way of the Charlatan - This one starts taking off as well with fool resistance and persuasion synergies.

Late Game
Living Deity - Gains 2 domains to choose from as a capstone capping an already strong Way.
Time Manipulator - Finishes by completely resetting one of your characters once per combat and even more spell resets.
Razmiri Infiltrator - Gains its counterspell ability making most casters a joke as they can only cast once per round.
Magic Inventor - Finishes by doubling the enhancement bonuses from equipment.
Way of the Charlatan - A bonus to spell fabrication not bad, but not amazing.
Fake Celebrity - The one way we haven't mentioned yet. This is my opinion, but this Way is hit or miss depending on party. It's anti-bard songs provide the enemy with lower saves/AC/AB/Damage, but when you get them tends to not be amazing in comparison to the other Ways.
Race Selection
Here we figure out the positives and negatives of specific races that will mesh with the Magical Deceiver.

Human - The advantages here are easy to see. You are able to pick your racial bonus attribute as charisma and you get a bonus feat.

Elf - You gain Elven magic which helps your early game spell penetration, but here that's mostly it. You don't gain a bonus to charisma for your casting and the Deceiver has no attack roll spells to use making the dexterity bonus limited to your crossbow. Do note the racial heritage "Loremaster" provides a bonus to Use Magic Device which might be helpful to the Magic Inventor.

Dwarf - Like the Elf this race provides very little to the Deceiver with attributes that don't really mesh with the archetype. That is not to say you can't find some use from playing one. The racial heritage "Barrow Dwarf" provides a Lore (religion) bonus which the Living Deity could potentially use.

Gnome - Here we have a race that fits well with the Deceiver. It's main attributes are Constitution and Charisma and gets Gnome Magic which boosts the DC of your Illusion spells by +1. It can also choose the racial heritage "Pyromaniac" for a +1 DC to fire descriptor spells instead.

Halfling - Like gnome this race receives a bonus to charisma which will aid in the DC checks of your spells as well as providing you a +1 bonus to all your saving throws and +2 to your dexterity for your crossbow when not casting. The racial heritage "Hasty" removes the halfling's speed penalty while granting a bonus +2 to initiative which is a solid choice.

Half-Elf - Half-Elf like human can pick up a racial attribute score of their choice as well as having several good racial heritages. Basic gives you a free skill focus which can be useful for several of your "Way of the Deceiver" features. Dual Heritage will let you reclaim Elven Magic for free for your early game casting needs. Kindred-Raised will let you obtain 21 or 22 starting charisma.

Half-Orc - Like human and half-elf the humble half-orc can also select a racial attribute. The intimidating racial trait gives a +2 bonus to persuasion checks synergizing with several Way of the Deceiver features. The Racial heritages here aren't amazing, but "Tribal" provides a +1 luck bonus to your saving throws.

Aasimar - Aasimar has several racial heritages to choose from.
  • Basic - Provides a +2 charisma attribute.
  • Angelkin - Provides a +2 charisma, and +2 Lore (religion) and +2 Use Magic Device bonus on top of granting a once per day Lesser Restoration.
  • Emberkin - Provides +2 charisma, +2 intelligence for more skill points, a +2 Lore (religion) skill bonus and can use the Burning Arc spell once per day.
  • Idyllkin - Provides a solid +2 constitution and +2 charisma and providing a once per day summon of Nature's Ally III.
  • Lawbringer - Does not give a charisma bonus, but does give a constitution and wisdom bonus for better saves and slightly more hp. It also provides a +2 bonus to Persuasion which synergizes with several Way features and has a free once per day Hold Person cast.
  • Musetouched - Provides a +2 bonus to dexterity and charisma, and gets a +2 bonus to Persuasion which will synergize nicely with several Way features. Can cast Glitterdust once per day.
  • Plumekith - Invisibility once per day not much else here.

Tiefling - An ok choice, but not the best.
  • Beastblood - Provides +2 dexterity and charisma. Can cast Blindness once per day.
  • Pitborn - +2 charisma and can use the stone call spell once per day.
  • Shackleborn - +2 charisma, +2 constitution and a +2 bonus to intimidation checks. Can cast web once per day.
  • Spritespawn - +2 dexterity and charisma, and provides a +1 bonus to DC with enchantment spells. Once per day sound burst.

Oread - Oread are ok, but also not the best.
  • Gemsoul - Provides +2 charisma and can use color spray once per day.

Dhampir - Another ok
  • Base - +2 charisma
  • Moroi-Born - +2 charisma

Kitsune - Kitsune can provide decent bonuses. Kitsune magic provides +1 DC to your enchantment spells and basic kitsune provides +2 dexterity and charisma.
Feat Selection
Here is where a lot of people will be stumped when creating their Magical Deceiver.

What Not to Choose

  • Metamagic feats - These are useless to you, your magic fusion replaces the metamagic menu.
  • Feats that improve your attack capabilities. You are a low BAB caster these will provide you next to nothing in benefits.
  • Skill feats that do not pertain to your Way of the Deceiver features.

Beneficial Feats

  • Spell Penetration - This will help you if your Way choice does not provide spell penetration at level 12 as well as aid in piercing early game spell resistant enemies such as demons. Is also needed to grab spell chain.
  • Spell Focus - Improve your spell DCs to make sure your spells work as often as possible.
  • Arcane Spirit - Your swift action is generally free and it uses Persuasion which is one of your best skills to lower enemy saves by 2 on the next spell cast.
  • Ambuscading Spell - Limited use -2 on flat footed enemies. Need greater invisibility or other sources of causing flat footed.
  • Deft Hands(Magic Inventor) - +2 or +4 bonus to Use Magic Device
  • Elemental Focus - Add +1 DC to element of your choice.(Think fireball or lightning bolt)
  • Dispel Focus - Adds a +2 to your dispel check when attempting to dispell.
  • Persuasive - +2 or +4 to your persuasion checks.
  • Allied Spellcaster - Useful if you have multiple casters to boost their spell penetration.
  • Improved Initiative - +4 bonus to initiative to help you cast first.
  • Skill Focus - Limited use to boost several Way features.
  • Spell Focus - Choose a school to focus and gain +1 to your DC
  • Greater Elemental/Spell Focus - Pick this if you got the previous versions. Gives another +1 to DC of the chosen type.
  • Favored Enemy Spellcasting(Advanced if you dip ranger for a favored enemy) - Your chosen favored enemy suffers a -2 to saving throws vs your spells.
  • Greater Dispel Focus - Can grab if you got the previous version. Provides another +2 the dispel checks.
  • Improved Elven Immunities(Elf/Half Elf only) - Provides another +1 to enchantment DCs.
  • Spell Chain(Teamwork feat) - Allows you or an ally to roll twice when attempting to pierce spell resistance
  • Spell Specialization - Pick a spell and it is counted as 2 levels higher for its features such as improving rounds per level by +2.

Mythic Ability

  • Abundant Casting and Improved Abundant Casting - This one is a no brainer. 4 extra spells per spell level.
  • Ascendant Element - Bypass elemental immunities and resistances.
  • Best Jokes - You have the spell so it's not a total loss.
  • Elemental Barrage - You cast 2 spells at once this will improve your damage output.
  • Last Stand - Extra Survivability if you need it.
  • Mythical Beast -Living Deity with animal companion revelation only.
  • Domain Zealot - Living Deity level 20 only.

Mythic Feats

  • Expanded Arsenal - expand your list of spells to fuse by gaining spell focus and school mastery benefits for another school.
  • School Mastery - Another +1 Caster Level to a school of your choice.
  • Extra Mythic Ability - Get your mythic abilities faster.
  • Extra Feat - Only really need this if you have nothing else to choose.
  • Sorcerous Reflex - Get a swift casted basic spell for free every combat.
  • Mythic Spell Focus - Another +1 DC of your chosen spell school.
  • Skill Focus Mythic - Good for boosting your way skill to always be a take 10. Works with features from your ways such as Spell Fabrication or Fool Resistance.
Tips
  • Focusing on using the ability "Intimidation" in the early game tends to work much better than trying to simply attack as you can lower damage taken, lower enemy accuracy, and lower enemy saves when the enemy can be shaken.
  • Use your Magic Fusion as often as possible you can fuse during combat, after combat, before combat and you have 10 spells with which to work with.
  • Make use of your spells that don't require saves or spell resistance checks when fusing.
  • Make use of rods as needed to cast your non-fused spells as needed such as swift casting a debuff and fuse casting a big damage dealing spell or crowd control spell.
  • Magical Deceiver can pick up Arcane Trickster and use the capstone to apply sneak attack damage to both fused spells essentially doubling your double damage.
  • Increasing your Caster Level will be a big boon towards your damage when you fuse 6th level spells. For example going from a 20d6/20d6 Chain Lightning/Fireball to a 31d6/31d6 Chain Lightning Fireball.
Example Build
This is an example of how to build using a Way of the Charlatan Deceiver.

Class: Magical Deceiver
Way of the Deceiver: Way of the Charlatan
Race: Human
Racial Heritage: None
Background: Street Urchin -> Pickpocket - For bonus initiative
Attributes:
  • Strength - 10
  • Dexterity - 16
  • Constitution - 10
  • Intelligence - 12
  • Wisdom - 10
  • Charisma - 19
  • Racial Bonus - Charisma
  • On level up - Charisma

Skills:
  • Persuasion - Used for the Archetype
  • Use Magic Device - Used for casting scrolls of spells you don't have
  • Lore(Nature) - Used for the world map to avoid fatigue
  • Stealth - Used to avoid aggro and if you have no other stealth focused companion

Deity: Any

Alignment: Any

Feats:
  • Arcane Spirit - Lower enemy saves by 2
  • Skill Focus(Persuasion) - Used for later as well as boosting our main tactics of Arcane spirit and Intimidation
  • Spell Penetration - Useful early on to get passed enemy spell resistance
  • Spell Chain - Useful to have for allies to get passed enemy spell resistance. It rerolls the spell resistance check for an ally when you attempt the check.
  • Spell Focus - Choose any school to gain a DC bonus to I like Abjuration as Dispel Magic pairs with nearly any spell. Evocation, Necromancy or Divination also make solid choices.
  • Greater Spell Focus - Further Improve your Focus.
  • Dispel Focus - Improve your dispels.
  • Greater Dispel Focus - Further improve your dispels.
  • Dispel Synergy - Improve your spell DCs after successfully dispelling.
  • Destructive Dispel - Cause Sicken or stun on successful dispel.
  • Anything you want - Pick anything here.

Mythic Abilities:
  • Ascendant Element - Bypass an elemental resistance(Useful for your elemental spells)
  • Abundant Casting/2nd Ascendant Element - 4 more spells per day for your 1st/2nd/3rd level spells.
  • Improved Abundant Casting/3rd Ascendant Element - 4 more spells per day for your 4th/5th/6th level spells.
  • Elemental barrage - Bonus Damage for fused elemental spells. Not incredibly useful, but damage is damage.
  • Last Stand - More survivability

Mythic Feats
  • Mythic Spell Focus - Further improve your school DCs.
  • Sorcerous Reflex - Free swift cast once per combat for your non-fused spells.
  • School Mastery - Improve a school.
  • Expanded Arsenal or another school mastery or anything really.
  • Anything you feel fits your build.

Mythic Class - From my testing Azata with Zippy Magic is easily the best choice for deceiver allowing all its spells to hit multiple times and proc elemental barrage multiple times per cast if you fuse 2 elemental spells together or allow you to CC an entire enemy group multiple times over.

How to use this build:

1. You have the ability "Intimidation" this is an infinite use ability that any character can get. This will apply a debuff on non-immune enemies called "shaken" shaken will lower enemy AB/Saving Throws/skill checks by 2 this will drastically lower the amount of damage a character takes from the enemy early on as well as a way to make it more likely for your spells to land on the target.
2. As you play you'll fuse spells for fairly strong combinations. An early example is combining Incendiary Runes with Ray of Sickening to make a spell that bypasses enemy spell resistance and AC while having the chance to apply a damage over time and apply the "sickened" condition to them. Sickened further reduces their damage/AB/Saving Throws/Skill Checks by 2. This spell combination can also pick reflex or fortitude for the saving throw so you can further improve your chances of it succeeding. Sleep is another good spell early to pair with your Ray as it can provide a will saving throw instead of reflex if that is lower(Do note this combo will not bypass spell resistance).
3. As you get further into the game you will obtain the feature "Fool's Resistance" this will help your spell penetration immensely as you add more ranks to persuasion.

Do note: This is just an example build and by no means the best build for this archetype. There are many ways to build it effectively.
Current Bugs Found
Several bugs currently with the archetype:

1. Fused spells do not inherit +damage per die effects such as dragon bloodlines bonuses.
2. Expanded Arsenal applies to both the original school and the new school doubling school bonuses.
3. Cha conversion of Living Deity still wonky for cha to Lore (Religion)
4. Picking Dragon Disciple lets you gain dragonbloodline spells to your spellbook.
